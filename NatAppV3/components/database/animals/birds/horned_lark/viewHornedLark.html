Horned lark

Overview

Horned larks are social birds that are usually found on the reserve outside of their breeding season. They prey on small insects and search for small seeds. The Horned Lark mixes with other open country species.

Identification

Horned larks are brown with yellow faces that have the outline of a black mask. They also has tiny black horns that are on the side of their heads. They are relatively small in size.

Habitat

The Horned lark is found in the open country. They remain in North America year-round. During their breeding season (the summer), they can be found in the northern United States.

Behavior

Horned larks are common birds to see during daylight. They are very active birds that stay in large flocks outside of their breeding season.

Diet and Predators

Horned larks eat seeds and insects. They feed their offspring mainly insects, such as grasshoppers, beetles, and caterpillars.

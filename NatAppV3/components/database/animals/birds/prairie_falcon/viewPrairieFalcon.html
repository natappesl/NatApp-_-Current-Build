Prairie falcon

Overview

The prairie falcon is frequently used in the sport of falconry. They are large in size for a falcon.

Identification

The feathers on their chests are white with brown spot/bars. Their faces are marked by what looks like a dark mustache that moves along the side of their beaks.

Habitat

In the summer, prairie falcons are typically found in:

  • Hilly Areas
  • Plains
  • Prairies
  • Grasslands
  • Deserts
In the winter, they can be found living in:
  • cultivated fields
  • lakeshores
  • desert and xeric scrublands
They avoid forested country and are rarly seen along the coast.

Behavior

Adult prairie falcons are extremely territorial during their mating season and will fight off intruding birds with loud sounds and attacks. The call of a prairie falcon is a loud "kree-kree-kree", which is usually heard when they are near their nests.

Diet and Predators

Prairie falcons mostly eat small birds and mammals. During their breeding season, their diet consists mostly of ground squirrels; during the winter when the ground squirrels become dormant, their diets shift to young songbirds.

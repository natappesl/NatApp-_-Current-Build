American Kestral

Falco sparvius

Overview

The American kestrel is North America’s smallest falcon. Look around for wooden kestrel boxes on the VPGR, which are in place to encourage kestrel breeding on the reserve.

Identification

This raptor is about the size of robin. The head is blue and orange-brown with two distinct black lines extending beneath the eye and on the side of the head. Males are identified by their blue wings and orange-brown backs. Females are orange-brown with distinct black barring throughout.

Habitat

American kestrels are found throughout the reserve most of the year, usually flying over on perched on the kestrel boxes.

Behavior

American kestrels will often be perched on fence lines or telephone wires while hunting. They can usually be spotted in flight because they will hover over a field before they dive down to catch a prey. Listen for a kestrel’s klee-klee-klee alarm call while on the VPGR.

Diet and Predators

Common prey items include grasshoppers, dragonflies, centipedes, beetles, voles, mice, and even lizards.

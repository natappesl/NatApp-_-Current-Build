Great Valley Button Celery

Erynigium castrense

Overview

The great valley button celery is a perennial and native species. Its thistles offer a defense against herbivory.

Identification

This plant is identified by its spiny protrusions, or thistles, between its purple and white sepals. Its stem is smooth and pale green to tan in color and stands no taller than 18 inches.

Habitat

This plant is typically found near vernals pools on the VPGR and blooms April through July.

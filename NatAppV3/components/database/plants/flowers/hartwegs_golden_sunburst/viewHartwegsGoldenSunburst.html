Hartweg's golden sunburst

Overview

Hartweg’s golden sunburst has a blooming period that is 3-4 weeks long and occurring in March and April. Their fruit is called an "achene" and is 2 mm. long.

Identification

Hartweg’s golden sunburst is an annual herb that grows 5-10 cm tall. Its leaves are linear or lance-shaped and they usually grow up to 2.5 cm. long and often have three small lobes near the tips of their leaves. They have one golden floret per phyllary, up to a cm. in length.

Habitat

Hartweg’s golden sunburst is only found in the Central Valley of California; the range of the species extends from Yuba County all the way south to Fresno County, a range of 200 miles. They became an endangered species in 1997 due to most of their native ranges being changed by agriculture.

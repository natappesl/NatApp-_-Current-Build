Owl's clover

Castilleja exserta spp. exserta

Overview

Owl’s clover is tube-shaped and has colorful bracts, which give it the appearance of an actual flower.

Identification

Owl's clover varies from 4-8 in. tall. It has a white, yellow, and purplish tip, which make it very distinguishable between other flowers.

Habitat

The grassy plains are the primary environment of owl’s clover. Owl's clover is located mostly in western Northern America, in places such as:

  • Northwestern California
  • Sierra Nevada Foothills
  • Great Central Valley
  • Central Western California
  • Southern California

Predators and Beneficial Organisms

Its colorful flowers attract butterflies and bees. Owl’s Clover is beneficial for the Bay Checkerspot butterfly which is a threatened species in California.

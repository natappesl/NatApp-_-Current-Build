Yellowflower tarweed

Holocarpha virgata, ssp. vigrate

Overview

Yellowflower tarweed is a native, non-invasive species to the reserve. Tarweeds are known for their sticky resin that they excrete through their stems and leaves.

Identification

Usually no taller than 1m in height; green stems and leaves, yellow flowers.

Habitat

Yellowflower tarweed is found throughout the reserve, typically in open areas near the edges of trails or roads. It blooms from May-November.

Predators and Beneficial Organisms

Small birds and mammals may eat the seeds of this plant.

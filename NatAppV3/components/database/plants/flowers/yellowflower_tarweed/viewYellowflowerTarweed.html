Yellowflower Tarweed

Holocarpha virgata, ssp. vigrate

Overview

The yellowflower tarweed is a native, non-invasive species on the reserve. Tarweeds are known for the sticky resin excreted through their stems and leaves, which trap potentially harmful insects.

Identification

Usually no taller than 1m in height; green stems and leaves, yellow flowers.

Habitat

Annual plant found throughout the reserve, typically in open areas near the edges of trails or roads. Blooms May through November.

Predators and Beneficials

Small birds and mammals may eat the seeds of the plant. Seasonal pollinators benefit from the flower.
